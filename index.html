Markov Chain Based Quote Generator

Deployment
Website Github
Markov Chain Github
Tech stack: Javascript, Express, HTML, CSS, git
Features:
  Generates quotes of varying lengths using a Markov Chain
  Node.js files which allow you to generate your own chains and quotes using the input of your choice
Development Environment: Solo

This site procedurally generates quotes in the style of John Danaher, based on a Markov chain generated from his social media posts. The Markov chain script is generalizable and could be used to generate chains and quotes from any input text. The website uses a precomputed Markov chain derived from Danaher's posts to generate the quotes.

Bio, or, why are you a programmer?

In 2015, I graduated from University of Chicago with a degree in Political Science, then promptly spent two years not using it. I did do a pretty wide range of other things, including but not limited to interning at The Innocence Project, being a TA at a Stanford academic summer program, and working as a ski instructor. I learned a lot from these jobs, including that I really loved the jobs where I could learn and grow and always be developing my skills. In the beginning of 2018, I started looking for a long term career with those kinds of opportunities, and several of my friends suggested programming.

I had no technical or computer science background, but I started teaching myself Javascript and rapidly fell in love with it. I loved the creativity of breaking down complex technical problems and solving them piece by piece, and I loved the way every project or algorithm I tried taught me something new or gave me a new way to look at old problems. However, I knew I'd learn more, faster, in a group environment with classmates and a curriculum, so I applied to a bootcamp called Fullstack Academy of Code.

Fullstack was a 17 week intensive program that taught full stack (obviously?) Javascript web development, focusing on Express, Sequelize, React, and Redux. Much as I learned from the technical parts of the program, the opportunities to do group projects and help or be helped by my classmates were amazing as well. I got a ton out of the program, and since it ended, I've been looking for full time work as a software engineer.
